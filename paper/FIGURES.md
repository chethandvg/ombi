## 🔬 Figure Descriptions

### Figure 1 — Error Probability Overlay
**The central result.** P(source has ≥1 error) vs k (where bw = k × w_min) for all
6 DIMACS road graphs. Shows the two distinct regimes: NE/USA rise steeply (steep sigmoid),
while BAY/COL/FLA/NW rise gradually. The V27 operating point (k=4) is marked.

### Figure 2 — Error Probability Subplots
Individual panels for each graph with fill-under-curve, first-error annotation, and
graph metadata (node count, w_min, source count). Good for appendix or detailed analysis.

### Figure 3 — NE Phase Transition
**The smoking gun.** Shows max errors per source (log scale) and total errors across
50 sources for NE. At k=16→17, max errors per source jumps from 2 to 2,270 — a
**1,135× explosion** in a single step. This is an error propagation avalanche where
one wrong settlement poisons downstream vertices cascadingly.

### Figure 4 — Boundary Sweep
Bar chart showing safe_bw and fail_bw (absolute and as multiples of w_min) across
all 6 DIMACS graphs. Highlights the massive variation: NE safe at k=4, COL safe at k=26.
V27's k=4 operating point is overlaid.

### Figure 5 — Conjecture Scorecard
Side-by-side comparison of actual safe_bw vs predictions from all 4 conjectures
(minCritBw−1, min(w_correct), min(gap), min(w_correct+1)). All predict 1–2, actual
is 8–54. Dramatic visual proof that no simple formula works.

### Figure 6 — Collision vs Error
Bar chart comparing same-bucket collision count vs actual error count at fail_bw.
COL has 11,171 collisions but ZERO errors — the ultimate proof that collision is
necessary but not sufficient. Error rates: BAY 3.54%, NE 0.25%, NW 2.48%, COL 0.00%.

### Figure 7 — Family 1 Ratio
Synthetic diamond graph: safe_bw vs w_correct (left) and their ratio (right).
The ratio varies from 1.5× to 75× — NOT constant. Disproves any linear relationship
between w_correct and the safe boundary.

### Figure 8 — Non-Monotonic Errors
Bar chart of error count vs bw for Family 3 (5 diamonds with different w_correct).
Green bars = 0 errors, red bars = >0 errors. Errors appear at bw=9, disappear at bw=13,
reappear at bw=14 — binary search for the boundary would fail!

### Figure 9 — Gap vs safe_bw
Family 5 (parameterized gap): larger gap → larger safe_bw, but in a staircase pattern
with jumps. Shows the complex relationship between error magnitude and boundary.

### Figure 10 — V27 Performance
V18 vs V27 speedup over DijkstraReusable2 across 4 datasets. V18 is faster but
incorrect on USA (❌). V27 trades ~20% speed for 100% correctness (✅ on all).

### Figure 11 — Version Evolution
The 27-version journey from V1 (baseline) through V18 (fastest, broken) to V27
(correct champion). Green = correct, red = incorrect. Shows the optimization phase
(V1–V18) and correctness quest (V19–V27).

### Figure 12 — Two Regimes
Side-by-side comparison: steep sigmoid (NE, USA) vs gradual rise (BAY, COL, FLA, NW).
Annotated with regime characteristics: steep = early errors, 100% quickly, avalanche
possible; gradual = late errors, never 100%, few errors per source.

### Figure 13 — Convergent Path Diagram
Visual diagram of the convergent path interference mechanism. Shows source → correct
path → p* → v and source → wrong path → p' → v, with the same-bucket condition and
LIFO settlement order requirement.

### Figure 14 — Error Heatmap
2D heatmap: graphs (rows) × k values (columns), colored by P_src. Clearly shows
NE/USA as hot (red) rows and COL as cool (green). V27 operating line at k=4.

### Figure 15 — Research Timeline
Visual summary of all 12 research phases with status indicators and key discoveries.
Shows the progression from design through optimization, correctness, theory, and
probabilistic characterization.

## 🎨 Style Notes

- **Color scheme**: Colorblind-friendly palette — each graph has a unique color+marker
- **Resolution**: 150 DPI (suitable for screen, presentations, and web)
- **For print**: Edit `figure.dpi` and `savefig.dpi` to 300 in the script
- **Font**: Default matplotlib (DejaVu Sans) — change to Times New Roman for paper

### Figure 19 — Three-Condition Formula *(PLANNED)*

space, showing how their intersection creates isolated error points. Data from
`DiagFifoFormula.cs`.

## 🆕 Figures 21–27: Δ-stepping, B_safe Hardness & Graph Taxonomy *(PLANNED — Phase 15-16)*

### Figure 21 — Δ-stepping vs LIFO vs FIFO on DIMACS *(PLANNED)*
**The headline figure.** Grouped bar chart: 6 DIMACS graphs × 3 policies (LIFO/FIFO/Δ-step),
showing total error count at k=50. Δ-stepping bars are all zero (green). LIFO and FIFO
bars show massive errors (NE: 108K LIFO, 83K FIFO). Data: `fifodimacs_summary.csv`.
**Paper §8.3, README hero figure.**

### Figure 22 — Δ-stepping Error Count = 0 Across All k *(PLANNED)*
Line chart: error count vs k (1..50) for NE (worst case), with three lines: LIFO (red,
rising steeply), FIFO (orange, rising), Δ-stepping (green, flat at zero). Shows the
dramatic separation. Data: `fifodimacs_ne.csv`. **Paper §8.3.**

### Figure 23 — LIFO vs FIFO First-Fail k on DIMACS *(PLANNED)*
Horizontal bar chart: 6 graphs, showing k at first LIFO error (red) and first FIFO error
(blue). Shows the 3-3 split: BAY/NW LIFO safer, FLA/NE/USA FIFO safer, COL FIFO zero.
Data: `fifodimacs_summary.csv`. **Paper §8.5.**

### Figure 24 — B_safe vs Graph Properties (Correlation Scatter) *(PLANNED)*
4-panel scatter plot: B_safe vs (n, m, w_min, w_max) for random graphs. All show weak
correlation (|r| < 0.21). Regression lines overlaid. Data: `hardness_properties_vs_bsafe.csv`.
**Paper §9.2.**

### Figure 25 — Graph Family Taxonomy: Non-Monotonicity Prevalence *(PLANNED)*
Horizontal bar chart showing non-monotonicity percentage by graph family: caterpillars (0%),
parallel paths (varies), grids (65-92.5%), random G(n,p) (100% at n=30-100), layered DAGs
(84.1%). Color-coded green→red. Data: `general_*` CSVs. **Paper §9.6.**

### Figure 26 — B_safe Decreases with Graph Size *(PLANNED)*
Line chart: average B_safe vs n (5..500) for random graphs. Shows the counterintuitive
decrease: larger graphs have lower B_safe. Error bars for variance. Data:
`general_bsafe_growth.csv`. **Paper §9.7.**

### Figure 27 — Grid B_safe Heatmap *(PLANNED)*
2D heatmap: grid size (3×3 to 20×20) vs B_safe. Shows the decrease from avg 129 (3×3)
to avg 16 (20×20). Data: `general_grid_bsafe.csv`. **Paper §9.6.**
